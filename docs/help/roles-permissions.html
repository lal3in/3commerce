Roles & permissions

Who can sign in and what they can do: the principal types, the gateway→claims→PDP/PEP authorization pipeline, the six built-in roles, the 24 code-defined permissions with risk levels, and the dynamic admin-managed RBAC (ADR-0025/0026). For sales or compliance conversations, pair this with Selling information so RBAC, service accounts, and MasterGlobal override are positioned with the right caveats.

Who can sign in — principals

Every request resolves to a principal in a tenant (ADR-0023). A person can be a principal in several tenants; a service account is a non-human principal (ADR-0026).

How a request is authorized

  1. The browser/CLI calls the gateway with the opaque 3c_session cookie (ADR-0012); anonymous requests pass through.
  2. The gateway introspects the session with Identity → (UserId, TenantId, Role, Email) and mints a short-lived ES256 internal-claims JWT (sub · role · sid · tenant · email) in the X-Internal-Claims header.
  3. The service validates that JWT (InternalClaimsAuth) and applies the coarse endpoint policy: CustomerPolicy (role customer or admin) or AdminPolicy (role admin).
  4. For fine-grained action/field decisions, the service acts as a PEP and asks the Identity PDP (PolicyDecisionService.DecideAsync), which resolves the principal's effective permissions from their tenant memberships → roles → permissions and decides per action/field (ADR-0025).

Dynamic RBAC + the admin screen

Roles are data: a principal's effective permissions are the union across their memberships' roles. Operators manage this on the Roles & permissions screen (and the /api/identity/admin/rbac API).

MethodPathPurpose
GET/admin/rbac/permissionsThe permission catalog
GET · POST/admin/rbac/rolesList / create roles
PUT/admin/rbac/roles/{id}/permissionsSet a role's permissions
POST · DELETE/admin/rbac/memberships/{mid}/roles/{rid}Assign / remove a role on a membership
GET/admin/rbac/principals/{pid}/effective-permissionsResolve a principal's effective permissions

Use cases

  • New finance hire: assign the finance role — they can view payments, issue refunds, and read the ledger + audit, nothing else.
  • Narrower ops role: clone ops into warehouse and drop mission_control.view.
  • Automation: create a service account for the CLI, grant catalog.product.edit, and rotate its credential on a schedule.
  • Emergency override: a MasterGlobal operator approves a maker-checker change across tenants — the action is written to the hash-chained audit log (now searchable via the Audit service).
  • Least privilege: a customer principal has no back-office permissions; the storefront only ever needs CustomerPolicy.
